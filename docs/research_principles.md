# Research Principles

Frontier Sky Collective follows a set of guiding principles intended to maintain transparency, rigor, and scientific integrity.

---

## Data Before Interpretation

Observations are documented and preserved prior to analysis or speculation.

---

## Preference for Conventional Explanations

Known natural or human-made explanations are evaluated first before considering unusual interpretations.

Examples include:

- aircraft
- satellites
- atmospheric phenomena
- sensor artifacts

---

## Multi-Sensor Correlation

Whenever possible, events should be verified using multiple independent data sources.

Examples:

- optical observation
- ADS-B aircraft data
- satellite tracking databases
- radio-frequency monitoring

---

## Transparency of Methods

Observation methods, equipment configurations, and analysis techniques will be documented openly.

---

## Preservation of Raw Data

Original observational data will be preserved to allow independent verification or future analysis.

---

## Reproducibility

Where possible, the project will prioritize tools and methods that can be reproduced by other independent observers.
